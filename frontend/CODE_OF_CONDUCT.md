# Code of Conduct

The Upper Room aims to be a respectful and practical open source community.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful and constructive.
- Focus feedback on the work, not the person.
- Assume good intent while still asking for clarity when needed.
- Welcome different experience levels and backgrounds.
- Respect maintainers' decisions about scope, priorities, and project direction.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, discrimination, threats, or personal attacks.
- Sexualized language or imagery.
- Trolling, sustained disruption, or bad-faith arguments.
- Publishing private information without permission.
- Any behavior that makes participation unsafe or hostile.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, contact the project maintainers through the repository's private reporting channel. If no private channel is available, open a public issue requesting a maintainer contact without including sensitive details.

Maintainers will review reports and may remove comments, close issues, reject contributions, or block participants when necessary.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to project spaces, including issues, pull requests, discussions, documentation, and community channels connected to the project.
